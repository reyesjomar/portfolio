# A Comparative Analysis of Maximalist and Traditional Running Shoes

## Overview
Examined whether there is a change in kinematics in relation to the type of shoe: maximalist or traditional an individual wears when running.

## Tools Used
- Excel
- Vicon Motion Capture

## Key Outcomes
1. Higher Knee Varus in Maximalist Shoes
- Participants wearing maximalist shoes showed greater peak knee varus (7.73째) compared to traditional shoes (7.35째), suggesting a higher risk of knee joint injury due to medial overloading.
2. Increased Ankle Eversion with Maximalist Shoes
- Ankle eversion was higher in maximalist shoes (7.60째 vs. 6.67째), which, while within normal range, could increase stress on the foot and raise the risk of conditions like plantar fasciitis or Achilles tendinitis.
3. Lower Loading Rate in Maximalist Shoes:
- Maximalist shoes reduced loading rate during running (57.3 BW/s vs. 75.7 BW/s in traditional shoes), indicating better shock absorption and potentially lower impact-related injury risk.
